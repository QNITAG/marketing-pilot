# Research brief

## Executive summary

As of 2026-07-13, Qnit can publish six practical core organic feed post formats from its LinkedIn Company Page: text-only, single-image, multi-image, document/PDF, poll, and link-preview posts. A Super Admin or Content Admin can create Page content; LinkedIn's Page instructions explicitly support text, URLs, photos, and documents, while LinkedIn's poll instructions explicitly permit Page admins to create polls.

The term "carousel" needs care. LinkedIn removed its native mixed image/video Carousel creation tool on 2023-06-26. Two still-current formats are often called carousels informally: (1) a multi-image post, which LinkedIn renders as an image layout, and (2) a document post, usually a multi-page PDF that readers page through in the feed. They have different production constraints and should not be treated as the same format.

Articles and newsletters are current Company Page publishing formats but sit outside the short-form feed-post taxonomy. Events and job listings can create or be shared through feed posts, but they are specialized objects with their own workflows. Reels, native video posts, LinkedIn Live, audio, and other video-first formats are outside this brief's scope.

All external sources below were accessed on 2026-07-13. Unless noted otherwise, source quality is **primary/official**: LinkedIn Help or LinkedIn Marketing Solutions Help.

## Communication objective

Give Qnit a reliable format menu for planning organic Company Page content without confusing active feed formats, long-form or specialized publishing objects, and retired or excluded video formats. The taxonomy should support later editorial decisions; it is not a recommendation to use every format or a draft of publishable copy.

## Audience insight

Qnit's LinkedIn audience includes engineering leaders, QA and test engineers, developers, and prospective team members. These groups need different depths of content: concise expert observations and questions in the feed; diagrams, event photography, and visual sequences for scanning; downloadable documents for structured knowledge; and articles or newsletters for durable, long-form expertise. Hiring and event content also matter, but they should be planned as specialized workflows rather than mistaken for ordinary media attachments.

## Recommended narrative

Treat the six core formats as a choice driven by audience task, not as cosmetic variants. Use text for a sharp point of view, one image for a single human or visual anchor, multiple images for real-world sequences, documents for structured teaching, polls for bounded questions, and links when the destination itself is the value. Use articles and newsletters only when depth or recurrence justifies the additional workflow. Keep events and jobs in their specialized creation systems, then support them with feed storytelling.

For Qnit's Expertise & insight pillar, prioritize document/PDF posts, concise text posts, and selective link previews. For Events & industry and People of Qnit, prioritize single- and multi-image posts with permissions and attribution. Use polls sparingly as conversation starters, not evidence of market prevalence. For Hiring, lead with the real team and work while linking every factual role claim to an active, approved vacancy.

## Unknowns and risks

1. **Qnit Page access is not verified in this research.** Confirm that the publishing user has Super Admin or Content Admin access.
2. **Newsletter eligibility is account-specific.** Confirm that Qnit has more than 150 followers/connections, an original-content history, good standing, and that `Create newsletter` appears in Admin view before planning a launch.
3. **Interface labels and staged tests can vary.** LinkedIn says it continuously tests features; verify the composer immediately before production, especially for mobile/desktop differences and third-party scheduling.
4. **Poll detail beyond the verified essentials is intentionally omitted.** The official pages verify Page support, 2-4 options, selectable duration, and a one-week default, but this research did not rely on unexpanded FAQ answers for character limits or every duration choice.
5. **"Carousel" is ambiguous in normal marketing speech.** Every Qnit brief should specify `multi-image post` or `document/PDF post`; do not request a generic LinkedIn carousel.
6. **Link previews depend on destination metadata and crawl access.** Test each final URL in the live composer; compliant Open Graph tags do not guarantee extraction if assets are blocked or protected.
7. **Scheduling differs by format.** LinkedIn's native Page scheduler excludes events, multi-photo posts, reshares, polls, jobs, and service posts. Do not assume Buffer or another third party has identical support without a separate current check.
8. **Job limits are dynamic and account-dependent.** The official Help page describes typical application caps and limited free postings; verify the actual vacancy workflow before promising duration or reach.

## Do not claim

- Do not claim that LinkedIn currently offers its retired native mixed image/video Carousel creation tool.
- Do not use `carousel`, `multi-image post`, and `document/PDF post` as interchangeable production specifications.
- Do not call a standard LinkedIn video a Reel or imply that LinkedIn Company Pages have a verified organic format named Reels.
- Do not claim that all Page post types can be scheduled natively.
- Do not claim that Qnit is eligible for Page newsletters until the option is confirmed in Admin view.
- Do not describe poll results as representative market research without a defensible sampling method.
- Do not promise link-preview appearance before testing the final destination and metadata.
- Do not imply that free job distribution is unlimited, permanent, or equivalent to sponsored reach.
