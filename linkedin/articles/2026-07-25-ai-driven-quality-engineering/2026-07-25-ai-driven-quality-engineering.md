# Why AI-driven quality engineering is the next wave

**Subtitle / deck:** AI won't replace testers — it will change what great testing looks like.

**Estimated read:** 4 min

## Introduction

At events like the Tricentis AI Tour, one theme keeps surfacing: AI is moving from
a novelty in quality engineering to a core part of how teams ship software. But the
hype often skips the practical question — where does AI actually create value in QA,
and where does human judgment still win?

## The problem AI is really solving

Test suites grow faster than the teams maintaining them. Flaky tests, brittle
selectors, and slow feedback loops quietly erode trust in automation. This is where
AI-assisted tooling earns its place: reducing maintenance, not just generating more tests.

## Three areas where AI is already delivering

1. **Test creation & maintenance** — self-healing locators and AI-suggested cases cut
   the busywork that makes automation expensive.
2. **Risk-based prioritization** — models that surface the highest-risk changes help
   teams test what matters most, first.
3. **Analysis at scale** — triaging failures and spotting patterns across thousands of
   runs is a job AI is genuinely good at.

## Where humans still win

Context, product intent, and "should this even work this way?" are not model problems.
The best quality engineering pairs AI's speed with human judgment about risk and users.

## How Qnit approaches it

As quality assurance specialists for cloud native, bespoke applications, and SAP, we
treat AI as an accelerator inside a disciplined engineering practice — not a shortcut
around one.

## Conclusion

The next wave of quality engineering isn't "AI vs. testers." It's teams that use AI to
spend less time maintaining tests and more time on the questions that actually protect users.
